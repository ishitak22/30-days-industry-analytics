# Dataset Selection

## Selected dataset

**HR Employee Attrition**

The selected dataset is a tidy employee-level HR analytics dataset focused on employee attrition and workforce characteristics. Each row represents one employee, making it suitable for interactive Power BI analysis across departments, job roles, demographics, compensation, satisfaction, tenure, training, overtime, and promotion history.

## Source

- Common source: IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition & Performance sample dataset
- Public dataset page: https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/pavansubhasht/ibm-hr-analytics-attrition-dataset
- Local file: `data/HR-Employee-Attrition.csv`
- Format: CSV
- Rows: 1,470
- Columns: 35

## Why this dataset was selected

This dataset is structured in a clean, tidy format with one row per employee and a wide set of HR variables. It is more suitable for dashboard development than aggregated workforce summary tables because users can slice attrition and workforce patterns by multiple dimensions, including department, job role, gender, age, overtime, income, satisfaction scores, training, tenure, and promotion history.

## Business problem it can help answer

The dataset can support a workforce analytics dashboard focused on understanding employee attrition risk and the employee characteristics associated with turnover. HR leaders could use the dashboard to identify which groups have higher attrition, whether overtime or satisfaction is linked with exits, and how compensation, tenure, promotion history, and job role relate to retention.

## Suitability for a Power BI portfolio project

This dataset is suitable for Power BI because it has enough variables to support multiple interactive views and realistic HR business questions, including:

- Which departments and job roles have the highest attrition rates?
- How does attrition differ by age group, gender, marital status, and education field?
- Is overtime associated with higher attrition?
- How do monthly income, job level, and salary increases compare across attrition groups?
- Do job satisfaction, environment satisfaction, work-life balance, and job involvement differ for employees who leave?
- How do tenure variables such as years at company, years in current role, and years with current manager relate to attrition?
- Are employees with fewer training opportunities or longer periods since promotion more likely to leave?
- Which employee segments should HR prioritise for retention planning?

The dataset is not Australian, but it is tidy, employee-level, and broad enough to build a polished HR attrition analytics dashboard in Power BI.
